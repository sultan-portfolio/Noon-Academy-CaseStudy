NOON CASE STUDY // BY SULTAN

What, Why, and Tomorrow.

What's happening: Learning gains exist, but outcomes vary widely because the system doesn't reliably convert effort into mastery, and classroom execution varies.

Why: 3 recurring patterns explain most of the variance:
  • Misguided Effort (high attempts, low accuracy, low gain) = High Churn Risk
  • High Potential / Low Structure Fit (high gain + attendance, low missions) = Retention Opportunity
  • Teacher Paradox (high engagement + trust, low mastery) = Brand Risk

What we do tomorrow: Build Detection + Adaptation loops that are simple, scalable, and move learning gains fast.

The north star and how does it shine

My North Star Metric is Learning Gain per Effort (LGpE), ensuring every 'click' in Noon translates into real 'knowledge'.

HOW TO CALCULATE
LGpE = (EOC - Diagnostic) / Effort

Effort = (Attempts% + Missions%)
Note: All values normalized

Why it matters?

  • Prevents busywork from looking like progress
  • Highlights hidden churn risk (high effort, low ROI)
  • Gives ops a clear intervention trigger

The Teacher Paradox / Teacher 368

Data is saying:
  • Extremely high engagement (95–99%)
  • Exit Ticket scores are low (~58%).
  • High Trust rates
What I see:
  • "Entertainment Bias." The experience is enjoyable
  • The learning remains superficial.
  • Students are trusting the moment not the outcome
What I’ll do:
1- Rebalancing Incentives: Tie teacher evaluations to a combined score
Engagement + Learning Outcomes. (One cannot exist without the other)
2- Micro-Assessment Injection: Embed 2–3 checkpoints during the session instead of relying solely on the end.
Simple “Best Achievers” scoreboard
3- Exit Ticket Threshold: Set a minimum acceptable threshold at 70%.
If scores fall below this: Trigger an automated review of the lesson material.
How I’ll measure it:
  • Raise Exit Ticket scores from 58% → 70%+.
  • Maintain Engagement above 85%.
  • Compare trust to the outcome
Bonus Note (Psychological/Human):
  • Peak-End Rule: facilitator focus on the “Peak” with joy, but leaves the “End” with unexpected results.

Guardrails: Assumptions & Risk Mitigation

Bold actions require sharp checks. My strategy assumes that data is the map, but the classroom is the terrain. We don’t just implement; we validate and pivot.

1. The Data Validation Phase:

Before launching any automated trigger, we must validate the "Noise" in the current dataset:

  • Attempt Integrity: Do "Attempts" include "retries" or "rapid-guessing" loops? We must filter for Genuine Cognitive Effort before flagging a student.
  • Level Normalization: Difficulty isn't linear. A 25% accuracy in Advanced Math is different from 25% in Level 1 English. Triggers must be Dynamic, not static.
  • Metric Validity: Is "Trust" a leading indicator of retention or a lagging indicator of comfort? We must correlate trust with LTV (Lifetime Value) before using it as a success proxy.
  • Student Segmentation: Onboarding questions are a starting point. Within 7 days, the system must Overwrite this classification based on Behavioral Data. We trust what the student does in the app more than what they say in the survey.

2. Operational Assumptions

  • The School Model: I assume School B represents our "Golden Standard" for execution. We use its benchmarks to audit School E’s friction points.
  • Tech Scalability: I assume the current stack can support Real-time Event Triggers (e.g., flagging a student *during* the session, not 24 hours later).
  • Facilitator Agency: I assume facilitators have the pedagogical training to act on the "Visibility Layer" data once provided.

3. Risk Mitigation (Goodhart’s Law Defense)

Every system designed to be measured is a system designed to be gamed. Here is how we protect Noon:

  • The Cobra Effect (Grade Inflation): To prevent teachers from "simplifying" Exit Tickets to boost their scores, we introduce Independent Spot-Checks.
  • The "Efficiency" Trap: To ensure the LGpE doesn't encourage "easy learning," we pair it with a Challenge Index. We want efficient mastery of *hard* concepts, not fast completion of *easy* ones.
  • Human Friction: Automated alerts can feel punitive. We frame interventions as "Support Sprints" to maintain the "Protocol of Calmness" and student trust.
  • 1:1 Human Escalations: 1:1 sessions are a Day-1 Discovery Tool, not a long-term scaling strategy. We use these sessions to gather qualitative data on 'Why' students fail, which then feeds into our Automated Adaptive Engine (AI-Tutor) for 100x scalability.
